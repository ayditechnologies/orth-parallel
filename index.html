An engine with nothing hidden, just condensed.

The experience surfaces ten findings. Behind them sits an engine that runs roughly a hundred-and-eighty validation points on every coordinate. Parallel shows the grower the top of the iceberg. The iceberg is real.

01
Seven-dimensional climate reading

Every coordinate is mapped across seven axes: annual temperature, coldest-month, hottest-month, diurnal swing, annual precipitation, winter-rainfall concentration, and elevation. This is the fingerprint. Two fields with the same fingerprint are climate twins, even if they sit on different continents.

02
A three-hundred-region reference pool

Parallel matches every submitted coordinate against three hundred curated agricultural regions spanning seventy-nine countries, from the Andalusian olive belt to the Ethiopian coffee highlands, from Okanagan orchards to Mekong rice. A minimum distance rule prevents the tool from returning a grower's neighbour as their twin.

  • MediterraneanSpain · Italy · Greece · Morocco · Lebanon
  • Southern HemisphereChile · South Africa · Australia · NZ
  • Tropical HighlandColombia · Rwanda · Yunnan · Dalat
  • Desert AgricultureRafsanjan · Al Ahsa · Xinjiang
03
Open public data, with fallbacks

Every coordinate queries ERA5 climate reanalysis through Open-Meteo as the primary source, with NASA POWER as an automatic fallback when the primary is rate-limited or unavailable. Soil properties come from SoilGrids ISRIC. Elevation from SRTM. The stack is deliberately public and reproducible.

  • Climate primaryOpen-Meteo · ERA5 reanalysis
  • Climate fallbackNASA POWER · climatology
  • SoilSoilGrids ISRIC · nine properties
  • ElevationSRTM · thirty-metre
04
Scoring that earns the word twin

Similarity is a weighted distance across the seven dimensions, with a Köppen-Geiger climate-class bonus when both fields share the same classification. A match below seventy-five percent similarity isn't called a twin. Parallel would rather say there's nothing quite like your field than overreach.

05
Cached and sourced

Each coordinate's reading is cached locally so repeat queries are instant. Every finding carries its own source attribution inline. If a data layer is unavailable for a given coordinate, Parallel says so instead of guessing.

Honest limits, stated upfront.

Parallel runs on publicly available climate and soil data. That choice is deliberate for the brand experience, and worth naming clearly so nobody mistakes the demo for the product.

01
Public data has a ceiling

ERA5 reanalysis and SoilGrids give global coverage at useful resolution, but they aren't field-scale. A twenty-five kilometre climate cell is a rough neighbourhood, not a vineyard row. Every finding in Parallel is accurate at the scale the data resolves, and no finer.

02
What a paid engine would add

Sentinel-2 imagery at ten metre resolution, hyperlocal forecast models, proprietary soil surveys, regional agricultural bulletins in four languages, disease-pressure networks like CABI and EPPO. These are the layers that turn a portrait into field-specific decision intelligence. None of them are in the brand experience by design.

03
What a million-user rollout would cost

Running Parallel globally at scale is an infrastructure conversation, not a brand one. Caching, coordinate quantisation, backend proxying, and a paid tier of the primary weather provider would move the unit economics to roughly thirty euros a month for the first ten-thousand users. Tractable at that scale, and worth budgeting for before the numbers grow.

04
What Parallel will never do

No recommendations. No spray decisions. No yield estimates. No crop-specific guidance beyond what the grower could read in any climate atlas. The brand experience stops exactly where the real product begins, on purpose.

Two surfaces, one voice.

Parallel lives where attention is short and curiosity is highest: the open website and the first minute of product onboarding.

01
The public website

Parallel is the marquee experience on askorth.com, a standalone route from the main navigation. Anyone can run it without an account. The shareable card generated at the end is indexable, public, and carries ORTH's mark.

02
Product onboarding

Inside the app, Parallel becomes the first screen after sign-up. Before the grower marks a plot, configures a crop, or answers any question, ORTH hands them their field portrait. The product proves itself before it asks for anything.

03
Campaign channels

Performance ads send traffic to Parallel, not to a sales page. Brand campaigns use individual Parallel readings as creative. Email invitations lead with a reader's own regional twin. Influencers in agronomy, viticulture, and speciality crops run the tool on camera and share the findings.

04
What we measure

Runs per region, time spent on the output card, share rate, secondary traffic from shared cards, and the conversion path from Parallel experience to plot creation inside the product. Parallel is the top of the funnel, measured honestly.

The quiet question, what if this becomes real?

This section is for the engineering side of the room. It acknowledges that Parallel, beneath the brand layer, is a genuinely novel piece of intelligence infrastructure.

01
Value in under thirty seconds

Parallel delivers a substantive reading of a field in less time than most agritech onboarding flows take to ask for a phone number. The engine can run at the front of the product experience permanently, as a genuine architectural advantage.

02
The bridge from Parallel to ORTH

When a grower wants more than what Parallel shows, the natural next step is: mark your plot. The brand experience doesn't push toward this. A grower who sees their field portrait and wants deeper context will look for the door themselves.

03
Parallel as a permanent capability

The engine can live inside the product too, as the first layer of context every time a grower opens a new plot. In that future, Parallel becomes a foundational tool. That's a decision for later, and a different document.

The case for building this seriously.

The test for Parallel is whether it makes ORTH unforgettable to the growers and agronomists who encounter it. Accuracy, craft, and design are the baseline, not the goal.

A brand experience that works at ten-thousand runs per month costs no more than a single performance-ad line. At a million runs per month, it reshapes how an industry hears about ORTH.
01
Earned attention

Parallel is engineered to be shared. Every run is a potential inbound: an agronomist posting their home region's portrait, a viticulturist sharing their vineyard's twin, a buyer running the tool on a sourcing region. The organic surface area is larger than any paid campaign can buy.

02
Brand density

Every Parallel reading carries ORTH's name, typography, and voice. Users remember it as a moment when a tool told them something specific about their own land. That kind of recall is hard to buy.

03
Credible entry to serious conversations

When ORTH enters a new market, a Chilean wine region, a Portuguese olive cooperative, a Mexican citrus district, Parallel is the first artefact that lands. By the time a real sales conversation happens, the brand is already familiar.
